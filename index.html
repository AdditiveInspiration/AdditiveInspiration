The first steps...on a long journey

3d Printing, April 7, 2017

Having just bought and built a new 3d printer we have decided to start a 3d printing blog! We're two engineers, trained in the same university but with very different skills - hopefully which will complement each other! Andrew is significantly more experienced with 3d printers and should be able to help reduce the steep learning curve it takes to become a 3d printing machine.

I'm excited - I have read every website, article, facebook group - which has prepared me for the frustrations that a cheap 3d printing kit can cause. We decided to buy a tevo black widow kit - It was well reviewed and relatively good value.

I'm pleased to report it was delivered quickly, in a very big and heavy box! Taking it out of the box I was holding my breath, expecting an incomplete kit of cheap and assorted parts. Wish us luck in the assembly and post any tips/experiences in the comments section below! I have included stock photos of the ultimate 3d printing benchmarks - the benchy and an xyz calibration cube! Hopefully our printer can produce similar results as shown above!

My First Print

3d Printing MAY 12, 2017

Expectations and reality are very different things...This first print has not been quite as successful as I had hoped. There are a number of things that need to be fixed and tuned. It can become hard to isolate the problem: this machine is a complicated beast.

The calibration cube is a good first print - it can help to isolate any problems with your printer. The stl for this particular file is available on thingiverse. Thingiverse Link

This blog/website is really going to be a slow burner...like all things..including 3d printing.. It is going to take time! Printing the calibration cube has shown me that. It would be great if anyone reading this would post any issues that they faced doing this print in the comments below!

Its nearly impossible to predict all of the issues that could be faced in one post but getting a community of people together to tackle issues would be really great!
