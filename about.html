Personal Profile: Robert Brennan

Biography 25th April 1993

After graduating, I started work in the Intel Wearables group where I worked as a DFx Engineer. The work involved utilizing system verilog, OVM, python and Perl to develop comprehensive tests for SOC designs. I had to communicate effectively with other teams. Under project timelines, it was important to constantly analyze and improve flows to make processes more efficient.

I graduated first in my class from University College Dublin where I obtained a Master's in Biomedical engineering. I won the EGA Crospon Biomedical engineering award for my academic performance and my final year project.

My final year project involved designing a subcutaneous near infrared vein visualization device. In order to succeed I had to quickly learn about optics utilizing near infrared light and lasers. Similarly I developed skills in image/video processing and wrote software in Python and c++ to achieve top marks. I worked for 8 months as an Engineer in Marturion Electronics as part of my Masters. I was given a great deal of responsibility within the company where I was given the opportunity to develop a wide range of skills. I was responsible for a project involving circuit design and software design. I worked with Pic and Arm microcontrollers where I used embedded C. I incorporated the Bluetooth and Ant+ wireless protocols into the final product. I built relationships with client companies throughout the project length to develop the best posible design solutions.
